About Me

I'm an interdisciplinary scientist by training, in areas of mathematical modeling, climate change, and dynamical systems. Most of my work has involved building *simple* models of complex systems and in developing visualizations to convey their mechanics and working. I have taught in college and universities for several years, and continue to teach graduate students currently. I have also worked in industry as senior data scientist and currently as a research manager at the NYT.

Communicating complex ideas and systems visually is an integral part of my work. This is also increasingly necessary in the information sphere to help us understand how to seperate truth from noise.

Some of my current/past projects, in reverse chronological order:
  • Building models of user behaviors
  • Building computer vision algorithms to track and analyze objects/organisms
  • Simulating the flow of macroalgae in the ocean, integrating with biological models
  • Modeling synchrony in bioluminscent organisms
  • Modeling periodic outbreaks of infectious diseases like small pox
  • Applying evolutionary algorithms to understand the dynamics of global trade
  • Building simple dynamical models of the Indian summer monsoon
  • Modeling cyclic climate variations
